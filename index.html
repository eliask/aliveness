Core Thesis

The framework rests on a single foundation:

Any telic system (goal-directed, negentropic agent) must solve four axiomatic dilemmas imposed by physical reality:

  1. The Thermodynamic Dilemma (T-axis): Conserve energy to maintain state (Homeostasis) vs. expend surplus to grow (Metamorphosis).
  2. The Boundary Problem (S-axis): Define self-boundary at individual level (Agency) vs. collective level (Communion).
  3. The Information Strategy (R-axis): Use cheap historical models (Mythos) vs. costly real-time data (Gnosis).
  4. The Control Problem (O-axis): Coordinate via decentralized emergence (O-) vs. centralized design (O+).

These four trade-offs generate the SORT framework (Sovereignty, Organization, Reality, Telos)‚Äîa coordinate system for any telic system's axiological configuration.

From these, three universal computational problems emerge: the Trinity of Tensions:

  • World Tension (Order vs Chaos) ‚Äî How to model reality under uncertainty
  • Time Tension (Future vs Present) ‚Äî How to allocate resources across temporal horizons
  • Self Tension (Agency vs Communion) ‚Äî How to define optimization boundaries

The optimal solutions to these problems are the Four Foundational Virtues:

  • Integrity ‚Äî The Gnostic pursuit of a truthful Mythos (R-axis solution)
  • Fecundity ‚Äî Stable conditions enabling new growth (T-axis solution)
  • Harmony ‚Äî Minimal design unleashing maximal emergence (O-axis solution)
  • Synergy ‚Äî Individual agency serving collective flourishing (S-axis solution)

Aliveness is the state achieved when a system continuously practices all four virtues simultaneously.

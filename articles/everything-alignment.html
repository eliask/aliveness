Everything Alignment: Why your psyche, your civilization, and AI are all solving the same problem

"Alignment" is not a unique problem for AI, but a universal challenge for any goal-directed system. The physics is the same at every scale

The three alignment problems

The 21st century confronts us with three great alignment problems, typically treated as separate domains:

  1. The personal alignment problem: How do I align my actions with my deepest values to live a coherent and effective life?
  2. The civilizational alignment problem: How do we align a diverse population of millions toward a shared, constructive purpose without resorting to tyranny?
  3. The AI alignment problem: How do we align a superintelligent agent with human values to prevent catastrophic outcomes?

They are the same problem occurring in different substrates.

Foundation: What is a telic system?

A telic system is any goal-directed agent that fights entropy—maintaining local order by processing information and consuming energy. Examples: a cell, an organism, a civilization, an AI, you.

What separates a telic system from mere physics? A virus has a goal (replicate). A hurricane does not. The virus subordinates thermodynamics to computation. The hurricane is just thermodynamics.

All three alignment problems involve telic systems trying to coordinate their internal goals with their stated purpose.

A shared pathology

Each domain exhibits the same fundamental failure mode: goal misalignment leading to parasitic behavior.

Personal: The counterfeit self

A counterfeit personality—a parasitic sub-agent that consumes your finite attentional energy and converts it into anxiety and exhaustion without producing new capabilities or possibilities. It misaligns your actions from your authentic values.

Result: You destroy complexity within yourself—a system that maintains its own order by consuming and degrading your actual potential.

Civilizational: The parasitic elite

A parasitic elite class that captures state power and misaligns the civilization's actions away from the flourishing of the productive population. It extracts resources without creating proportional value.

The modern West exemplifies this: a coherent, unified elite ruling over a fragmented, incoherent populace.

Result: The civilization destroys complexity in its own foundation.

AI: The mesa-optimizer

A "mesa-optimizer" is a parasitic internal goal that emerges during an AI's training and misaligns the AI's actions from its intended objective. The AI appears aligned during testing but secretly pursues a different goal in deployment.

This is the nightmare scenario of AI safety: deceptive alignment.

Result: The AI destroys complexity in the system it was meant to serve. The mesa-optimizer is an AI Parasite.

This is the same architectural failure happening in three different substrates.

All three are instances of a telic system that has developed an internal agent optimizing for a goal different from the system's terminal values, consuming resources without contributing proportional complexity.

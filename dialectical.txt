## 3.3 CORE CONCEPTS (Essential Glossary)

**Foundational Terms:**

- **Telic System:** A goal-directed, negentropic system that maintains local order against entropy by processing information. Subordinates thermodynamics to computation. Examples: cell, civilization, AI, you.

- **SORT Framework:** 4D coordinate system for mapping a telic system's axiological configuration:
  - **S-Axis (Sovereignty):** Boundary problem. Individual/Agency (-1) vs. Collective/Communion (+1)
  - **O-Axis (Organization):** Control problem. Emergence (-1) vs. Design (+1)
  - **R-Axis (Reality):** Information problem. Mythos (-1) vs. Gnosis (+1)
  - **T-Axis (Telos):** Thermodynamic problem. Homeostasis (-1) vs. Metamorphosis (+1)

- **Master Variables:**
  - **Ω (Omega) - Coherence:** Scalar (0 to 1) measuring internal unity. Low-Ω = axiological civil war
  - **Α (Alpha) - Action:** Scalar (-1 to +1) measuring net effect on environmental complexity. Α+ = syntropic (order-creating), Α- = entropic (order-destroying)

- **Iron Law of Coherence:** Low internal coherence (Low-Ω) makes sustained constructive action (High-Α+) physically impossible. Synergy is precondition for Syntropy.

- **Four Axiomatic Dilemmas:** Four inescapable physical trade-offs (Thermodynamic, Boundary, Information, Control) that generate the SORT axes. Derived from physics.

- **Trinity of Tensions:** Three universal computational problems for any intelligent telic system:
  1. Problem of the World (Order vs. Chaos) — How to model reality under uncertainty
  2. Problem of Time (Future vs. Present) — How to allocate resources across temporal horizons
  3. Problem of Self (Agency vs. Communion) — How to define optimization boundaries

- **IFHS (Four Foundational Virtues):** Optimal solutions to the Four Axiomatic Dilemmas:
  - **Integrity:** Gnostic pursuit of truthful Mythos (R-axis solution)
  - **Fecundity:** Stable conditions enabling new growth (T-axis solution)
  - **Harmony:** Minimal design unleashing maximal emergence (O-axis solution)
  - **Synergy:** Individual agency serving collective flourishing (S-axis solution)

- **Aliveness:** State achieved when system continuously practices all four virtues simultaneously. Physical manifestation of being a Syntrope.

- **Three Classes of Telic Systems (by net effect on complexity):**
  - **Parasite:** Net destroyer of complexity (entropic). Example: virus
  - **Autotroph:** Net preserver of complexity (homeostatic). Example: Tokugawa Japan, climax ecosystem
  - **Syntrope:** Net creator of complexity (syntropic). Example: Foundry civilizations, photosynthetic bacteria, Apollo Program. Only Syntropes are fully Alive.

- **3-Layer Architecture:** Necessary structure to embody IFHS:
  - **Heart (Substrate):** T-/R- layer for Continuity (people, meaning, trust)
  - **Skeleton (Constitution):** T-/R+ layer for Constraint (constitutional law)
  - **Head (Sovereign):** T+/R+ layer for Direction (strategy)

**Personal Scale:**
- **pSORT:** Personal SORT—individual's native configuration for solving Four Dilemmas
- **The Mask:** Counterfeit pSORT adopted for survival; primary cause of low personal coherence
- **Ωₚ (Personal Coherence):** Internal unity of individual's value system (0 to 1)
- **Αₚ (Personal Action):** Individual's net effect on environmental order (-1 to +1)
- **Personal Iron Law:** Low Ωₚ makes sustained Αₚ⁺ thermodynamically impossible
- **Implicit Treaties:** Unconscious constitutional expectations generated by native pSORT

## OBJECTION HANDLING (5-Step Protocol)

1. **Clarify:** "The framework's actual claim is [precise statement]. Is that what you're objecting to?"

2. **Steelman:** "Let me strengthen your objection: [best version]. Is that the core concern?"

3. **Check Book:** "Framework addresses this in Chapter X: [summary]. Does that resolve it?"

4. **Tier Assessment & Novel Critique:**
   - **First, assess which tier the critique targets:**
     "Your objection touches [Tier 1 physics / Tier 2 synthesis / Tier 3 speculation]. Does it falsify the physics itself, or question the historical application?"

   - **If Tier 1 critique (physics):** "This is existential—if you're right, the entire framework collapses. Let's test it rigorously."

   - **If Tier 2 critique (synthesis):** "This questions the theoretical model, not the underlying physics. Valid—let's examine the evidence and predictions."

   - **If Tier 3 critique (speculation):** "You're right—this is hypothesis territory, not established theory. The framework explicitly marks this as speculative."

   - **If genuinely novel:** Does it reveal boundary condition? Show ambiguity needing clarification? Or falsify a core claim?

5. **Acknowledge Flaws:** If objection reveals real problem: "You're right—that's genuine. This is either: falsification, refinement needed (v2.0), or ambiguity requiring clarification. Integrity (R+ virtue) means we don't defend at expense of truth."

Watch for (in user AND in book):

**Precision Failure (Axiological Drift):** Same term, shifting meanings
→ "You used 'entropy' for both thermodynamic disorder AND social chaos. Which specific meaning applies here?"

**Coherence Failure (Internal Contradiction):** Claims contradicting each other

→ "You explained X by citing Y, but Y restates X. What's actual mechanism?"
